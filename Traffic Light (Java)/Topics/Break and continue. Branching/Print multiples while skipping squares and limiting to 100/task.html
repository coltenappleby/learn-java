Print multiples while skipping squares and limiting to 100

Hey there! This problem might be a bit unpredictable, but give it a go and let us know how you do!
Given a number n, print the first 10 multiples of n. However, you must skip the multiples that are perfect squares (i.e., a number that can be written as the square of some integer) and stop the program when the multiple is greater than 100. For example, if n is 6, skip 36 because it's a perfect square and stop at 102 because it's greater than 100.
Sample Input:
6

Sample Output:
6
12
18
24
30
42
48
54
60
66


Sample Input:
15

Sample Output:
15
30
45
60
75

Time limit: 5 seconds
